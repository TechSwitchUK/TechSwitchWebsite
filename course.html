Who it's for

Techsmiths is for people from any background who want to make the move into the tech sector.

For example, you might be…

  • Working in another sector and wanting a career change, perhaps for a better work-life balance
  • Just out of university or college, and keen to be part of the vibrant tech industry
  • On a career break, and looking to return to work in a way that fits round your life

What you get from Techsmiths

  • A free 12-week bootcamp, to train you in the basics of software development and begin to deep-dive into programming languages that will help you hit the ground running on your placement. We also provide training in agile ways of working, DevOPs, software design, testing and relevant soft skills (Location: Kentish Town, London)
  • A personal mentor: Changing careers is a big leap to make. To support you on this journey, we provide a mentor, who will keep in touch with you throughout bootcamp and your placement, giving career advice and taking care of your wellbeing
  • A 15-month paid industry placement, where you’ll be part of a development team, writing code and building up valuable software engineering skills and experience (Location: London)

Once you complete your placement, the skills and experience you will have gained will put you in high demand in the job market. You may have the opportunity to continue your career at the organisation where you do your placement. Alternatively, you’ll be ideally placed to secure one of the myriad tech sector jobs in London and beyond.

Why Techsmiths?

Firstly, our training bootcamp is completely free, removing one of the biggest hurdles people face when considering a career change.

Second, your paid placement gives you financial security and priceless industry experience.

Third, we have a strong focus on diversity and hire based on potential rather than solely experience.

And fourth, you’ll be taught by a team of people who work at the cutting edge of the tech sector, day-in, day-out. We’ve built software to stream Glastonbury’s headliners. We’ve helped shape key UK government cloud computing roadmaps. We worked with DeepMind and Moorfields Eye Hospital to use artificial intelligence to diagnose sight-threatening eye diseases more quickly.

You’ll learn first-hand what it takes to build software that can change lives and improve the world we live in.

And you’ll be in good company. We’ve almost 20 years’ experience in training people to become outstanding software engineers. These individuals have gone on to work for organisations such as DeepMind (owned by Google’s parent company Alphabet), Anthony Nolan, Square Enix and END. menswear.
